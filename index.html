Frequently Asked Questions

What if we don't get enough survey responses?

▼

This is a common concern, which is why the audit is a multi-source analysis. If survey response rates are low, we simply increase the statistical weight of the other four data sources: the in-person observation, online review analysis, business context interview, and your internal data. This ensures the final diagnosis is always robust, comprehensive, and not dependent on any single point of failure.

What kind of results can we realistically expect?

▼

We promise diagnostic clarity and an actionable strategy, not a specific revenue guarantee. The value is in knowing exactly what to fix and why. However, our process is designed to identify the highest-impact, lowest-effort fixes. If you implement the top three prioritized actions from our report, you should expect to see measurable improvements in customer retention and feedback within 60-90 days.

Is this a big financial risk for a small business?

▼

We see this as an investment in keeping the customers you've already paid to acquire. The cost of losing just 2-3 loyal customers a month is often far greater than the entire one-time cost of the audit. Our goal is to give you a positive return on investment by plugging the leaks in your customer journey that are silently costing you thousands in repeat business every year.
